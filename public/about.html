Our History

Blooming Mind was established in 2022 in Jenks, Oklahoma by Ashley Woltjer. Since then, we have been committed to improving mental wellness and supporting families and individuals in need of mental health therapy services, as well as providing clinical supervision to several licensed professional counselor candidates (LPC-Cs) in northeast Oklahoma. Over the years, we have helped countless individuals achieve better mental health and emotional well-being.


At Blooming Mind, our mission is to create a safe, supportive, and inclusive space where clients feel empowered to explore their thoughts, emotions, and experiences. Through compassionate care and clinical expertise, we partner with our clients to foster growth, healing, and to reach their individualized goals.


Meet Our Team

Ashley Woltjer MS, LPC-S, NCC

My name is Ashley, and I am a mental health therapist and the owner of Blooming Mind PLLC in Jenks, Oklahoma.


As a Licensed Professional Counselor Supervisor (LPC-S) and National Certified Counselor (NCC), I work primarily with children and adolescents who struggle with behavioral, emotional, and communication difficulties related to autism, ADHD, sensory processing difficulties, and trauma responses.


I use an individualized approach with each client based on their specific needs and goals, but I naturally lean into a humanistic theoretical framework focusing on congruence, empathy, and unconditional positive regard throughout each session. I offer traditional talk therapy as well as a specialized variety of therapeutic approaches including interactive play therapy, art therapy, and infant mental health modalities, and animal-assisted therapy activities.


I believe that empowerment and comfortability drive people to create growth and change, so I strive to provide a safe environment for my clients to feel comfortable exploring their thoughts, expressing their feelings, and shaping their own values and worldviews. I assist in fostering diversity, advocacy, and inclusion with unconditional respect to all.


I obtained a Bachelor of Arts in psychology from Northeastern State University in 2016, a Master of Science in Counseling with an emphasis on clinical mental health from Northeastern State University in 2019, and a Master of Philosophy in Psychology from Walden University in 2024. I am currently a doctoral candidate at Walden University and am working toward completing a PhD in Psychology degree.


Ashley is in network with most insurance plans including Aetna, BCBS, Cigna/Evernorth, First Health, Health Choice, Healthcare Highways/90 Degree Benefits, Medica, United Healthcare (UHC), and United Medical Resources (UMR). Ashley also accepts private pay.

Emily Stabler MS, LPC-C

Hi, I'm Emily!


Taking the first step toward therapy isn't always easy, and I'm so glad you're here.


Our minds can be really convincing. Maybe anxiety keeps you caught in endless "what if" thoughts, depression has made it difficult to recognize yourself, or past experiences continue to affect your present in ways you didn't expect. You may feel overwhelmed, stuck, or simply tired of carrying everything on your own. Whatever brings you here, you don't have to navigate it alone.


I believe meaningful change begins with feeling seen, accepted, and supported. Therapy is more than learning coping skills—it's having a place where you can slow down, make sense of what you're experiencing, and show up exactly as you are. You don't need to have the perfect words or know where to start. Whether you need a space to laugh when it feels right, cry when you need to, ask hard questions, or simply be yourself, I'm here to meet you where you are. Therapy doesn't require perfection—it simply asks for a willingness to begin.


I enjoy working with adults navigating anxiety, depression, trauma, ADHD, and body image concerns. My approach is warm, collaborative, and tailored to each person's unique needs. I integrate evidence-informed techniques from Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT), Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT), and Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT) to help clients better understand themselves, develop practical coping strategies, and move toward the life they want to live. I believe therapy isn't one-size-fits-all, and together we'll find an approach that feels right for you.


Healing doesn't mean life suddenly becomes easy. It means learning to respond to life's challenges with greater self-compassion, confidence, and resilience. My hope is that therapy becomes a space where you feel supported, challenged when you need it, and reminded that growth is possible—even on the difficult days.


Wherever you're starting from, I'd love to walk alongside you as we work toward the life you want to build.


Emily is in network with Oklahoma medicaid (Soonercare/SoonerSelect) plans and accepts private pay.
